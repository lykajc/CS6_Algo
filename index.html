IBFO-ODLAD: Intelligent Bacterial Foraging Optimization with Simulated Annealing for Anomaly Detection

Advanced Feature Selection and Classification for Network Intrusion Detection

This study presents IBFO-ODLAD, an enhanced bacterial foraging optimization algorithm integrated with simulated annealing for automated anomaly detection. The proposed approach combines the exploration capabilities of BFO with the probabilistic acceptance criteria of SA, achieving superior performance in feature selection and classification accuracy across multiple benchmark datasets.

Baseline vs. Enhanced Algorithm
Baseline

Standard BFO

Bacterial Foraging Optimization

  • Chemotaxis: swim and tumble via flagella (Eq. 3)
  • Swarming: cell-to-cell attraction and repulsion (Eq. 4)
  • Reproduction: worst half eliminated, best cloned
  • Elimination-dispersal with probability Ped
  • Greedy acceptance — improve-only moves
  • Prone to local optima on high-dimensional data

Time Complexity O(Nre · Ned · Nc · M)

Enhanced

Improved BFO (IBFO)

BFO + Simulated Annealing

  • All BFO operators retained unchanged
  • SA Metropolis condition injected at chemotaxis
  • Accepts worse solutions with prob. exp(-ΔE/T)
  • Temperature cools geometrically: T ← T · α
  • Probabilistic escape — improves global optima
  • Optimized for non-convex feature landscapes

Key Takeaway

SA Enhancement Drives Superior Performance

The Simulated Annealing enhancement in IBFO reduces classification error and selects fewer, more informative features than standard BFO. On UNSW NB-15, IBFO-ODLAD achieves 98.89% accuracy (testing) — surpassing SSA-CRNN (98.25%) and VLSTM (95.73%) — while selecting only 19 of 42 features. On UCI SECOM, 98.66% training accuracy outperforms SSA-CRNN (97.62%) with 231 of 591 features selected (Khayyat, 2023).
